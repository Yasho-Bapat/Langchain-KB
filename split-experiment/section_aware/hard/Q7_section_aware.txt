Question: Which is more hazardous, Copper Sulphate or Ammonium Hydroxide?
Answer:  

Copper Sulphate is more hazardous than Ammonium Hydroxide. Copper Sulphate is considered hazardous by the 2012 OSHA Hazard Communication Standard (29 CFR 1910.1200). Copper Sulphate is classified as Acute oral toxicity Category 4, Skin Corrosion/irritation Category 2, Serious Eye Damage/Eye Irritation Category 2, Specific target organ toxicity (single exposure) Category 3, and Specific target organ toxicity - (repeated exposure) Category 2. Copper Sulphate is harmful if swallowed, causes skin irritation, causes serious eye irritation, may cause respiratory irritation, and may cause damage to organs through prolonged or repeated exposure. Copper Sulphate is very toxic to aquatic life with long lasting effects.
