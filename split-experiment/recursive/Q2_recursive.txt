Question: What are the hazards associated with 341D Belts?
Answer:  Answer: The 341D Belts have no hazard classifications according to OSHA Hazard Communication Standard, 29 CFR 1910.1200. The product has no signal word, symbols or pictograms. No hazards are identified beyond those associated with normal industrial hygiene practices.  The product has no hazardous materials as classified under the criteria of the Federal OSHA Hazard Communication Standard 29 CFR 1910.1200. The product does not have a WHMIS hazard classification. None of the components are listed as a carcinogen or potential carcinogen by IARC, NTP or OSHA. The product does not contain any substance listed as an extremely hazardous substance or toxic chemical. The product does not contain any Class 1 or Class 2 ozone depletors. The product is not a marine pollutant. The product does not contain any RCRA hazardous wastes. The product does not contain any CERCLA hazardous substances. The HMIS Hazard Classification is Health: 0 Flammability: 1 Physical Hazard: 0 Personal Protection: X-See PPE section. HMIS Hazardous Material Identification System ratings are based on inherent properties of the material under expected conditions of normal use and are not intended for use in emergency situations.
