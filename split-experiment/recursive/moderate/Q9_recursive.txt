Question: Provide a risk assessment for handling and storing large quantities of Copper Sulphate.
Answer:  Answer: Copper Sulphate is considered hazardous by the 2012 OSHA Hazard Communication Standard. It is harmful if swallowed, causes skin irritation, causes serious eye irritation, may cause respiratory irritation and may cause damage to organs through prolonged or repeated exposure. It is classified as a toxic material under WHMIS Hazard Class D2B. It is very toxic to aquatic organisms, may cause long-term adverse effects in the aquatic environment. It is recommended to wear personal protective equipment when handling it. Containers should be kept tightly closed in a dry, cool and well-ventilated place. It should be kept away from incompatible products and excess heat. It is important to ensure adequate ventilation, especially in confined areas. It is important to handle it in accordance with good industrial hygiene and safety practice.
