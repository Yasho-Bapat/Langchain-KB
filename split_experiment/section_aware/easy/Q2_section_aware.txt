Question: What are the hazards associated with 341D Belts?
Answer:   Answer: The product is not classified as hazardous according to OSHA Hazard Communication Standard, 29 CFR 1910.1200. There are no hazardous hazards not otherwise classified. The product is considered to be non reactive under normal use conditions. The product is stable. There are no hazardous reactions that will occur. There are no conditions to avoid. There are no incompatible materials. There are no hazardous decomposition products. Dust from grinding, sanding or machining may cause irritation of the respiratory system. Signs/symptoms may include cough, sneezing, nasal discharge, headache, hoarseness, and nose and throat pain. Mechanical Skin irritation: Signs/symptoms may include abrasion, redness, pain, and itching. Mechanical eye irritation: Signs/symptoms may include pain, redness, tearing and corneal abrasion. Dust created by grinding, sanding, or machining may cause eye irritation. Signs/symptoms may include redness, swelling, pain, tearing, and blurred or hazy vision. No health effects are expected. Cancer of the lungs has been observed in rats that inhaled high levels of titanium dioxide. No exposure to inhaled titanium dioxide is expected during the normal handling and use of this product.
