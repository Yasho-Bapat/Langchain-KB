Question: Analyze the regulatory discrepancies for Ammonium Hydroxide in different countries and what is the UN number assigned to it for transport purposes?
Answer:  Answer: Ammonium Hydroxide is present in the Pennsylvania Right-to-Know Hazardous Substances and New Jersey Worker and Community Right-to-Know Components. It is not listed in California Proposition 65. It is present in the Canada Domestic Substances List / Non-Domestic Substances List (DSL/NDSL) and the United States of America Toxic Substances Control Act (TSCA) List. It is not listed in the European Inventory of Existing Commercial Chemical Substances (EINECS), European List of Notified Chemical Substances (ELINCS), and No Longer Polymers (NLP). Ammonium Hydroxide is not listed in the Superfund Amendments and Reauthorization Act (SARA) 302 Extremely Hazardous Substances. It is listed in the Superfund Amendments and Reauthorization Act (SARA) 311/312 Hazardous Chemicals. The UN number assigned to Ammonium Hydroxide for transport purposes is UN2672.<|im_end|>
